Awareness & Access
Q1 · Awareness

How aware are you of the L&D team?

Opportunity: 20 respondents (21%) rated awareness 1–2. Boosting visibility for new joiners is critical.
Q2 · POC Knowledge

Do you know your L&D point of contact?

Gap: 35% don't know their POC. A simple "Meet your L&D team" onboarding email could close this fast.
Q3 · Discovery Channels

Where do you look for L&D info?

Slack is #1 discovery channel (66 mentions). The Landing and Email follow. 7 people said they don't know where to look.
Communication & Platforms
Q4 · Communication Quality

Clarity & frequency of L&D communication (1–5)

55% rated 4–5, but 21% rated 1–2 — suggesting a split audience where some are well-informed and others feel left out.
Q5 · Platform UX

Ease of navigation on OktaU / Cornerstone / Udemy (1–5)

70% rated 4–5 — platforms are generally navigable. Focus effort on the 7% who rated 1–2.
Participation
Q6 · L&D Participation

Participated in L&D programs in the last 6 months?

61% participated in the last 6 months. 39% (37 people) did not — a significant engagement gap to address.
Q7 · Barriers to Participation

Why didn't non-participants join? (n=37 who said No)

"Unaware of offerings" edges out "lack of time" as the top barrier — a comms and discoverability fix, not a scheduling one.
Content Relevance & Learning Needs
Q8 · Content Relevance

How relevant are current L&D offerings to your day-to-day work? (1–5)

55% rated 4–5. But 40% gave a neutral 3, hinting that content feels generic rather than wrong — role-specific tracks could shift this.
Q9 · Desired Training Areas

Which areas do you want more training in? (multi-select)

AI Fluency & Agentic Tools dominates with 75 votes (80% of respondents). Technical Skills and Leadership complete the top 3.
Learning Style & Growth Impact
Q10 · AI Comfort Level

Current comfort / fluency with AI tools

47% Intermediate, 37% Proficient/Expert. Strong foundation — AI training should skip basics and focus on agentic & applied use cases.
Q11 · Preferred Learning Format

What is your preferred format for learning?

Self-paced + In-person together dominate (62%). Only 10% prefer micro-learning — but demand for it correlates with time scarcity.
Q12 · Perceived Impact

L&D resources contributed to my performance & growth

80% agree (Agree + Strongly Agree). Only 8% disagree — a strong signal of value, though neutral responses (34%) suggest room to make impact more visible.

What's Working Well
✅
Strength

L&D is Making a Real Impact

  • 180% agree L&D directly contributed to their performance — only 8% disagree.
  • 2Content quality lands well with those who engage — the problem is reach, not relevance.
  • 330+ respondents left unprompted kudos, praising Learning Day, internship programs, and the team by name.
📢
Strength

Slack & Email Are Proven Channels

  • 170% use Slack as their #1 source for L&D info — the highest of any channel by a wide margin.
  • 2Email newsletters are a solid secondary channel at 45 mentions.
  • 3No need to build new channels — double down on what's already working.
🖥️
Strength

Platforms Are Accessible

  • 170% rated platform navigation 4–5 out of 5 — the UX is not the bottleneck.
  • 2The issue is knowing what to take, not how to navigate.
  • 3Aware employees convert to active learners at a high rate — discovery is the missing link.
🤝
Strength

Once Found, the Content Resonates

  • 1Employees with high L&D awareness (Q1 rated 4–5) show high participation — the offering works when it's discovered.
  • 2Participants gave largely positive relevance scores — the portfolio broadly aligns with engaged learner needs.
  • 3Qualitative sentiment is warm and enthusiastic across the board.
What's Not Working
🔍
Critical Gap

Awareness is the #1 Barrier — Not Time

  • 135% don't know their L&D POC; 21% rated awareness 1–2 — invisible to all current outreach.
  • 2"Unaware of offerings" is the top non-participation reason (17 people), beating "lack of time" (15).
  • 37 respondents don't know where to find L&D info at all — an onboarding gap, not a comms one.
🎯
Critical Gap

Content Feels Generic, Not Role-Specific

  • 140% rated relevance a neutral 3 — not rejecting it, just can't connect it to their role.
  • 2Engineers asked for business context; Finance flagged that morning sessions clash with their 2–11pm shift.
  • 3A targeting problem, not a content quality one — role-based paths would shift this fast.
🤖
Urgent Gap

AI Demand Far Outpaces Current Supply

  • 180% of respondents want AI & Agentic training — the largest gap across all 18 categories.
  • 2Specific asks: Agentic AI, Claude Code, MCP servers, RAG, prompt engineering, AI productivity.
  • 384% are already Intermediate+ — skip the basics, go deep and applied.
⏰
Systemic Gap

Learning Has No Protected Time or Structure

  • 1Employees need permission to learn, not just access — "make AI training mandatory so we actually do it."
  • 2Learning is self-initiated with no formal plan — outcomes are inconsistent as a result.
  • 3Multiple respondents independently requested a recurring, no-meeting learning block.
🗂️
Experience Gap

Too Many Platforms, No Clear Map

  • 1OktaU, Cornerstone, Udemy, LearnLUX — employees don't know what each is for or where to start.
  • 2No unified session calendar exists; upcoming events are scattered across channels.
  • 3Technical roles want O'Reilly, Pluralsight, or KodeKloud — cited independently by 5+ respondents.
Recommendations
⚡
Quick Win · High Impact

Run an "L&D Welcome" Awareness Campaign

  • →One Slack post mapping each team to their L&D POC — one day of effort, closes the 35% gap.
  • →Add an L&D orientation to new joiner onboarding; plugs the "I had no idea this existed" cluster.
  • →Monthly Slack digest with all upcoming sessions + one-click sign-up — requested by multiple respondents.
🤖
Strategic · Urgent

Launch a Tiered AI Fluency Track

  • →Three tiers: Applied AI (intermediate), Agentic AI & tool-building (advanced), Responsible AI (all). Skip the intro — 84% are already past it.
  • →Ground it in tools employees use: Claude, MCP servers, prompt engineering, RAG, AI productivity.
  • →Make one AI module mandatory per quarter — employees want the permission, not just the access.
🗺️
Strategic · Medium Term

Build Role-Based Learning Paths

  • →4–5 persona journeys: Engineer, People Manager, Business Analyst, New Joiner, Senior IC.
  • →The 40% neutral relevance score is a targeting problem — role-based filtering lifts it without new content.
  • →Surface on The Landing and pin in role-specific Slack channels.
📅
Operational · Engagement

Protect Learning Time

  • →Propose a monthly manager-endorsed no-meeting "Learning Hour" — even 60 min/month signals intent.
  • →Normalise 1 course/quarter as a soft expectation, with manager buy-in.
  • →Offer IST-friendly time slots — current morning-heavy schedule locks out Finance and late-shift teams.
🧩
Platform · Experience

Clarify Platforms & Expand Sources

  • →Publish a "which platform for what" one-pager: Cornerstone = compliance, Udemy = skills, OktaU = Okta-specific, LearnLUX = financial. Pin it everywhere.
  • →Add O'Reilly and one lab-based platform (Pluralsight / KodeKloud) — cited by 5+ respondents independently.
  • →Build a unified session calendar (Notion or Slack canvas) for a single view of all upcoming events.
🏆
Culture · Long-Term

Make Learning Visible & Rewarded

  • →Add lightweight recognition — Slack shout-outs or badges for course completions. Social proof compounds.
  • →Introduce a certification reimbursement policy (AWS, K8s, Okta certs) — flagged as a key motivator that's currently absent.
  • →Showcase L&D wins at town halls — several employees said they didn't know what the team does until this survey.
